01 — Sound becomes image

Every frame you see is a reading of the air.

There is no video here and no audio file. A lush ambient piece is synthesised live the instant you press begin — then handed, sample by sample, to an AnalyserNode. It runs a Fast Fourier Transform, splitting the signal into a spectrum of frequency bins many times a second. Those numbers are the paint.

/ LOW

Bass breathes the core

The lowest bins drive the diameter and heat of the central body. When the sub pulse swells, the whole bloom inhales.

/ MID

Mids sculpt the petals

The arpeggio and pad energy bend the radial curve — the spectrum plotted in polar space, mirrored into a living, symmetric flower of light.

/ AIR

Highs ignite the motes

Filtered noise and the top octaves brighten a field of drifting particles. The shimmer you see is literally the treble.

02 — The instrument

A small orchestra of oscillators, no recordings.

The piece is a patch, not a track. Six layers evolve against each other through slow low-frequency motion, so it never quite repeats — an infinite, gently drifting sound-bath assembled from raw waveforms.

LAYER 01 · PAD

Detuned drone

Stacked oscillators tuned to an open fifth, each split into a pair a few cents apart, poured through a slowly-opening low-pass filter.

LAYER 02 · ARP

Gentle arpeggio

A soft triangle voice walks the mode, plucked note by note with a short envelope — the melody that carries the mood.

LAYER 03 · AIR

Filtered noise

White noise through a wandering band-pass — the breath and hiss that gives the room its size and its top-end sparkle.

LAYER 04 · SUB

Slow pulse

A sine an octave below the root, swelling on a tremolo LFO — the heartbeat that the core of the visual answers to.

LAYER 05 · LFO

Slow movement

Sub-hertz oscillators glide the filter cutoff and detune so the timbre is always breathing, never static.

LAYER 06 · MIX

Analyser tap

Everything meets at a master bus, tapped by the AnalyserNode. The same signal you hear is the one you watch.

03 — Four rooms of light

Choose a mood. The key, the tempo and the palette all move.

Each mood re-tunes the instrument to a new root and mode, shifts the arpeggio's pace, and re-paints the bloom. Switch anytime — the transition is seamless.
