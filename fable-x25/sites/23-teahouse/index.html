The Water 水

Everything begins with what falls on the mountain.

Snowmelt takes eleven years to pass through the granite above us. It arrives at our spring with almost nothing in it — which is to say, perfect. We do not filter it. We wait for it.

The Room 間

Eight mats, one window, and nothing that asks for attention.

The window is placed where the mountain looks best in November.

The Way 道

We believe slowness is not the absence of speed. It is the presence of attention. A cup poured at the right temperature, at the right moment, for the right person, is the fastest thing we know — it arrives exactly on time.

So the house keeps almost nothing. A kettle, four teas, the sound of water. What remains when everything unnecessary is gone is not emptiness. It is room — for the season, for the guest, for the hour you did not know you needed.
