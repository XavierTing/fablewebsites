五行 · The Five Elements
先天八卦·THE EIGHT IMAGES

The Eight Trigramseach a number, an image, an element

In the early-heaven arrangement every trigram carries a number, one to eight. Divide any number by eight and the remainder names the trigram — zero returns to Earth. Two of them stacked make your hexagram.

占法·THE METHOD

How a Number Becomes a Hexagrammod eight, mod six, and the moving line

一

Two trigrams the upper & lower

The first number, taken mod 8, raises the upper trigram (上卦); the second the lower (下卦). A remainder of zero is Earth, the eighth. Stack them and the sixty-four appear.

二

The moving line 動爻

The sum of the numbers, taken mod 6, marks one of the six lines as moving — the single place where the present is already turning into something else.

三

Self & situation 體用

The trigram holding the moving line is the 用 (yong) — the situation. The still trigram is the 體 (ti) — you. Their five-element relationship gives the verdict at a glance.

四

Present & change 本 · 互 · 變

The cast hexagram (本卦) is now. Its inner lines two-to-five form the 互卦, the hidden middle. Flip the moving line and the 變卦 shows what it becomes.

體用生克·THE VERDICT

Five Ways the Moment Turnshow ti meets yong

Between your element and the situation's runs one of five relationships — generating (生) or overcoming (克). This alone tells favorable from adverse.

用生體
Most favorable
The situation feeds you — help arrives unasked. Move with it.
體用比和
Harmonious
Same element. Self and moment are of one mind; things run smooth.
體克用
Favorable, effort
You master the situation — but by force of will. Push, and it yields.
體生用
Draining
You pour yourself out. Much given, little returned. Better to hold.
用克體
Adverse
The moment presses on you. Avoid its edge; wait, then move.
