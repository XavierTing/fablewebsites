    No. I — The Claim

    Nothing on this instrument is drawn. Everything is solved.

    Most diagrams of the solar system are pictures — planets parked where a designer liked them. This one is an instrument. Set it to any date between 1600 and 2400 and each jewel is placed by the same celestial mechanics an almanac uses: six orbital elements per planet, aged by the centuries, passed through Kepler's equation, projected onto the plane of the ecliptic.

    Spin the flywheel and you are not playing an animation. You are re-deriving the sky, sixty times a second — Mercury's hurry, Neptune's patience, the Moon turning through her synodic month, retrogrades blooming and unwinding as Earth overtakes the slower worlds.

    No. II — The Mathematics

    One equation, iterated to silence.

    Kepler, 1609 · Astronomia Nova

    The equation with no algebraic answer

    A planet sweeps equal areas in equal times, so its clock-angle — the mean anomaly M — grows perfectly uniformly while the planet itself does not. The angle that locates it on the ellipse, the eccentric anomaly E, is bound to M by one short line that cannot be inverted in closed form. It has been solved by iteration for four centuries. It is solved by iteration here, at every frame.

    M = E − e·sin E

    Kepler's equation · transcendental in E

    II · Newton, four strokes

    Eₙ₊₁ = Eₙ − Δₙ
    Δₙ = (Eₙ − e·sin Eₙ − M)
        ÷ (1 − e·cos Eₙ)

    Seeded with E₀ = M + e·sin M, Newton–Raphson converges quadratically; for every planetary eccentricity a handful of strokes reaches 10⁻¹² radians — far below the width of any engraved line on this plate.

    III · Onto the ecliptic

    x′ = a(cos E − e)
    y′ = a√(1−e²)·sin E
    λ = atan2(y, x)

    Coordinates in the orbital plane are rotated through the argument of perihelion, the inclination, and the ascending node onto the plane of Earth's orbit. The longitude λ is what the instrument engraves: a bearing in the zodiac.

    No. III — The Long Watch

    Great conjunctions, found — not remembered.

    Every 19.86 years Jupiter laps Saturn and the two brightest wanderers stand together in the evening sky — the great conjunction, the slow bell of the solar system. The instrument does not look these up. When this page loads it walks its own Kepler pipeline forward day by day, watching the angular separation as seen from Earth, and lists the meetings it finds.

    No. IV — Honesties & Provenance

    What is true

    Every longitude, every zodiac reading, every retrograde flag, the lunar phase, and every conjunction date is computed from the element table — nothing is keyframed, cached, or drawn by hand. Bearings are true to a fraction of a degree across the instrument's range.

    What is feeling

    The orbit rings are spaced logarithmically, or Mercury would huddle invisibly against the Sun while Neptune left the room. Jewel sizes flatter the small worlds. Orbits are engraved as circles; the planets ride them at their true longitudes, eccentricity and all.

    How it is made

    One HTML file, one table of eighteenth-century-grade numbers, and vector engraving — no images, no data files, no lookup services. Set the wheel spinning and leave the tab; the instrument rests when unwatched, as any good movement should.
