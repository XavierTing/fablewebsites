銘香 · The Blends

Four sticks, four weathers

Each blend is ground, kneaded and pressed by hand in lots of a few hundred sticks. They burn slowly, speak quietly, and are priced for what they are: years of wood, weeks of drying, minutes of smoke.

聞香の作法 · How to Listen

Four movements, none of them hurried

  1. 一

    Light

    Hold the tip to a small flame until it takes, then wave the flame out. Only the ember should remain — smoke, not fire, carries the scent.

  2. 二

    Wait

    The first minute belongs to the binding wood. Set the stick down and let the room fill of its own accord.

  3. 三

    Listen

    Do not lean in. Let the air bring the scent to you, and attend to it as you would a quiet voice. This is why we say incense is heard.

  4. 四

    Remain

    When the stick has gone out the scent stays a while — in cloth, in hair, in memory. What it says then is part of the blend.

由緒 · The House

Ten generations, three rooms

The house was founded in the first year of Hōei — 1704 — by a paper merchant’s son who traded a season of washi for a chest of agarwood and could not bring himself to sell it. Ten generations have kept the same three rooms on Muromachi street: one for grinding, one for pressing and drying, one for listening. Recipes are corrected, never replaced; the fourth generation’s notebook is still consulted, its margins now deeper than its pages.

We make incense slowly, in small lots, for people willing to sit still. Nothing we sell will fill a room quickly. Everything we sell will change one.
