ELASTIC

Type was never meant to hold still.

Scroll — the argument unfolds Cursor = pressure  ·  proximity = weight
00 — Preface Fraunces · opsz 9→144
On the end of
frozen letters

For five hundred years we cast our alphabet in metal and called the stiffness craft. A letter weighed what the foundry said it weighed. It leaned only when a punchcutter allowed it to lean. The word sat on the page like a fossil — beautiful, and finished, and dead.

The variable font ended that. A single file now holds every weight between hairline and black, every width between crushed and sprawling, every degree of lean. A letterform is no longer a picture of a shape; it is a range of possible shapes, waiting for pressure. What follows is not a specimen. It is a demonstration of a material.

01 — The Manifesto Four axes · scroll-driven
01 · wght 100 → 1000wght 100

A letter should gain weight the longer you look at it.

Weight is not a property. It is a temperature — and attention is heat. This sentence is set at hairline 100 when it enters the page and reaches black 1000 before it leaves.

02 · wdth 25 → 151wdth 25

Language
stretches.meaning survives the pull

Compress a word and it whispers through a crowd; expand it and it fills a room without raising its voice. Width is volume for the eyes.

03 · slnt 0 → −10slnt 0.0°

Italics are not a style — they are a direction of thought, and a sentence can change its mind one word at a time.

Watch the lean travel through the line as you scroll: each word tips ten degrees forward, the typographic equivalent of leaning in.

04 · SOFT 0 → 100 · WONK 0 → 1soft 0 · w0

Perfection is a rumor started by rulers.

Fraunces carries two confessions in its file: SOFT, which melts every corner, and WONK, which lets the letters remember they were once drawn by a hand. Scroll, and watch the serif forgive itself.
