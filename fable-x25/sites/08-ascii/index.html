    SEC/01
    
THE MATHEMATICS OF LIGHT, TYPESET

    

  

  
No triangles. No GPU. Every frame above is computed the way it was done when the display was the terminal: a cloud of points on a parametric surface, spun by rotation matrices, sorted by a z-buffer, and lit by a single dot product — then quantized into type. Brightness becomes ink density. That is the whole engine.


  

    

      STAGE 01 — SURFACE
      
PARAMETRIC POINTS

      
The torus is swept from two angles: θ walks the small cross-section circle, φ carries that circle around the axis of revolution. Each (θ, φ) pair yields a 3-D point and — crucially — its surface normal, which comes free from the same trigonometry.

      x = (R₂ + R₁cosθ)·cosφ
y =  R₁·sinθ
z = (R₂ + R₁cosθ)·sinφ
    

    

      STAGE 02 — SPIN & SORT
      
ROTATION + Z-BUFFER

      
Three rotations — spin, tilt, precession — tumble the cloud each frame, the way a gyroscope wobbles on its bearing. Points project to the character grid through 1/z perspective. A z-buffer keeps, per cell, only the nearest point — so the far wall of the donut hides correctly behind the near one.

      ooz = 1 / (K₂ + z)
col = cx + K₁·ooz·x
row = cy − ½K₁·ooz·y   // cells are 2:1
    

    

      STAGE 03 — LUMINANCE
      
THE CHARACTER RAMP

      
Lambert's law, one multiply-add per point: λ = N̂ · L̂. The result indexes a ramp of glyphs ordered by how much ink they lay on the cell. Dense glyph, bright surface. The ramp is the palette.

    SPECIMEN/01 · PROPAGATION
    
FIRE, ONE ROW AT A TIME

    
The oldest trick in the demoscene: heat is just an integer. The bottom row of the buffer is pinned to maximum temperature; every frame, each cell steals heat from the cell below it, minus a small random loss, drifted one column left or right by the wind.

    
That single rule — copy up, cool down, jitter sideways — produces turbulence, licking tongues, and ember decay with no fluid dynamics at all. Here the temperature field is quantized twice: once into the glyph ramp, once into three phosphor intensities layered on the tube.

    
Cut the FUEL switch and watch it starve · GUST kicks the wind

  







  

    SPECIMEN/02 · AUTOMATON
    
CONWAY, ON THE TUBE

    
Four rules, no exceptions: a live cell with two or three neighbours survives; a dead cell with exactly three is born; everything else returns to vacuum. From that austerity comes traffic — gliders, oscillators, and the gun below, discovered by Bill Gosper in 1970, firing a glider every thirty generations.

    
Dying cells leave a brief phosphor ghost — the same persistence a real P31 tube would give you, done here with a second, dimmer text layer one generation behind.

ON TEXT-MODE GRAPHICS

        
Before the framebuffer, the character generator was the only brush. Teletext painted weather maps at 40×24. PETSCII artists on the Commodore 64 built portraits from quarter-blocks. The demoscene turned VGA text mode into a competition category of its own — and the tradition never died, it just moved: to BBS door art, to htop, to the donut.c that still ships in every systems programmer's heart. PHOSPHOR is a museum piece that runs at 60 frames per second.
