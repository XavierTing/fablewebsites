№ 03 — The Craft
I / IV

The size

Carrageenan — Irish moss, boiled and strained — thickens the water until it will carry pigment on its back. The bath rests overnight and is skimmed each morning with a strip of newsprint. It should pour like single cream.

II / IV

The colours

Earth and mineral pigments ground with ox-gall. The gall lowers each drop's surface tension so it spreads — and every new drop shoulders the older ones aside. The whole geometry of marbling begins in that little push.

III / IV

The comb

Brass pins set in beech, a different rake for every pattern: the nonpareil comb at a scant five millimetres, the wide feather comb, the single stylus for freehand veins. One pass, one direction, no second thoughts.

IV / IV

The pull

Alum-mordanted paper is laid down from the near edge in one unhurried roll, then pulled off with the whole pattern clinging to it. Rinsed, hung, pressed flat. What the paper takes, the bath forgets.

№ 04 — Commissions

Edition work, quietly done

We marble to order for binders, conservators and private presses: endpapers matched to a leather swatch, edition runs to two hundred sheets, edge-marbling for text blocks, and sympathetic repairs to papers older than this house. Send the book — or the idea of it.
