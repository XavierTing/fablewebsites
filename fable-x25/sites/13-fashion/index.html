VANTABLUME

Collection 001 — Exoskeleton

The Looks

Armour grown, not sewn.

01

Carapace Gown

Moulded bridle leather over bias-cut gazar — twelve plates, wet-formed on the body, riveted by hand. The corset closes with a single spine of blackened steel. 340 hours.

02

Thorax Column

Nine thousand lacquered scales, each cut from horn-pressed acetate and sewn singly to a tulle ground. The dress weighs eleven kilograms and moves like water. 520 hours.

03

Chrysalis

Articulated limb of vegetable-tanned hide on a jointed cane armature; crêpe de chine gown buttoned through with oxidised silver. Worn once, then archived. 410 hours.

“We do not dress the body. We give it a second skeleton, and let it decide what to become.”
Mireille Vantablume — Founder, 1987

Manifesto

The House

Vantablume was founded on a refusal: that softness is the only language a garment may speak. In the atelier on Rue de Poitou, cloth is treated as a living material with a will of its own — boiled, pressed, moulded, persuaded into structures that hold the air around the body like held breath.

Collection 001, Exoskeleton, studies the insect wing and the beetle's shell — surfaces that are armour and ornament at once. Every plate is formed by hand over plaster casts of the house's fit models; every scale is sewn by a single petite main from first to last, so that each dress carries one person's patience inside it.

Nothing here is seasonal. Nothing is repeated. Each look exists as a single exemplar, numbered, archived, and released only to those who understand that couture is not bought — it is entrusted.

The house shows once a year, in silence, without music. What you hear on the runway is the garment itself: the dry click of leather plates, the hush of gazar. This is the sound of Vantablume.
