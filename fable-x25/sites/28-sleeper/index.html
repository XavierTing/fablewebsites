About this line

The Sleeper departs every evening from a platform no timetable agrees on, and it has never once arrived. This is not a failure of the service. It is the service. Berths are made up with heavy wool blankets; the corridor smells of coal smoke and cold apples; the lamp above your pillow burns amber all night, because the dark outside deserves an audience.

Out the window the country repeats itself the way a lullaby does — plains, pines, lake, mountain, sea — each verse slightly re-painted. Passengers are advised to give up on sleep gracefully.

CARRIAGE NOTES

  • The lamp above berth 4C flickers when crossing water. This is normal, and some say lucky.
  • The dining car serves black coffee until the mountains, and warm milk after them.
  • Passengers wishing to be woken at dawn should tell the conductor, or the button below the window, whichever answers first.
  • Lost property is kept for one full journey. As the journey does not end, nothing is ever truly lost.
