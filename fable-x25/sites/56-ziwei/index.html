A thousand years ago the star-masters drew a life as a house of twelve palaces 十二宮 — Life and Wealth, Career and Spouse, Travel and Fortune — and set the wandering stars into their rooms. 紫微斗數 begins at the Purple Star 紫微, the emperor of the northern sky, and arranges the rest around it. Enter a birth and the chart is cast in real time: the lunar date resolved, the 五行局 derived, and the fourteen major stars placed room by room — the way a fortune-master would draw it on parchment.

III推盤之法How This Chart Is Cast
① 農曆 · Lunar date

Solar → lunarthe moon's month

The Gregorian date is converted to the Chinese lunar calendar with a bundled month-length table (1920–2040), giving the lunar month and day — and the year's Heavenly-Stem & Branch 干支.

② 命宮 · Life Palace

Month & hourthe anchor

From 寅 count forward by lunar month, then back by the birth hour — the palace you land on is 命宮. Counting the other way fixes 身宮, the Body palace.

③ 五行局 · Element Class

Nà-yīn2·3·4·5·6

The Life Palace's stem-branch gives its 納音 element — Water-2, Wood-3, Metal-4, Earth-5, or Fire-6 — the divisor that sets where the Purple Star begins.

④ 紫微 · Purple Star

Class & daythe emperor

The lunar day divided by the Element Class, adjusted by the classical 起紫微訣, seats 紫微. Its mirror across the 寅–申 axis seats 天府.

⑤ 十四主星 · Main stars

Fixed offsetstwo families

The 紫微系 (six) fall back from 紫微 by fixed steps; the 天府系 (eight) run forward from 天府. Twelve palaces are then laid counter-clockwise from 命宮.

⑥ 四化 · Transformations

Year stem祿權科忌

The birth-year stem grants four stars their 化祿·權·科·忌 — fortune, power, merit, friction — and the Element Class sets the 大限 decades, their direction by stem-polarity & gender.

A note on honesty, in the spirit of the almanac: the lunar conversion, the twelve palaces, the Five-Element Class, and all fourteen major stars plus 左輔右弼文昌文曲 and the four 四化 are computed faithfully from the classical rules and verified against a known chart. What a full reading adds — the hundred-odd minor stars 雜曜, each star's brightness 廟旺利陷, the flowing yearly & monthly charts — is summarised here, not exhaustively drawn. The lunar year turns at 正月初一 (not 立春); a leap month is read as its base month; the hour uses clock time without longitude correction. Where schools differ (the 庚-year 四化 especially), one common table is used.
