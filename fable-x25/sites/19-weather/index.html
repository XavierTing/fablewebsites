01 · The practice

Nephology is the study of clouds. This is a place to practise it slowly.

Everything on this page is drawn from the real atmosphere — the temperature two metres above the ground, the speed of the wind, the fraction of sky the clouds have claimed. Nothing is a photograph. The scene is generated, moment by moment, from the same numbers meteorologists read: a sky rebuilt from its measurements, moving at the pace of actual weather. Leave it open on a second screen. Let it change without telling you.

02 · Present readings

Four numbers, read closely.

Temperature
—
Measured two metres up, where a standing person keeps their face. The one number everyone asks the sky for first.
Wind
—
The clouds in this scene drift at this exact speed, scaled to the viewport. When the air is still, so is the page.
Cloud cover
—
The share of the dome currently held by cloud. Zero is an unbroken blue; one hundred is a ceiling of pewter.
Humidity
—
How much of itself the air is willing to admit. Past eighty percent, rain stops being a question of if.
03 · The repertoire

Ten skies live in this engine. Try one on.

Each state below is a full atmosphere — its own palette, light, particle field and pace. Ordinarily the weather chooses. Here, briefly, you may.
