Approach

02 — Method

On Mass

A wall should feel like a decision that cannot be reversed.

We begin every project with the heaviest possible version of the idea — a single volume, unopened, sitting on its site like a sentence with no commas. Only then do we permit ourselves to remove material. Each opening must be argued for; each cantilever must earn its structural anxiety.

The studio works in load-bearing masonry, board-marked concrete, and rammed earth wherever the ground allows it. We distrust cladding. A building whose surface tells the truth about its section needs no further ornament.

On Void

The rooms we are proudest of contain almost nothing.

Emptiness is not absence — it is the most expensive material we specify. A double-height void costs a floor of programme; a courtyard costs a wing. We spend that budget deliberately, placing silence where a client's life will most need it: above a bath, beside a reading chair, at the exact turn of a stair.

Every project in the field is drawn at 1:200 before it is modelled, and drawn again by hand after it is built. The distance between those two drawings is where we keep our conscience.
