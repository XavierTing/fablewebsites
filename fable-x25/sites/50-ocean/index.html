an open sea

Stand at the bow. The water goes on without asking, and the low sun lays a road of light straight across it — right to where you stand.

— The reading

The horizon is not a place you arrive.

It is a promise the sea keeps by never keeping it. You steer toward it for a lifetime and it holds its distance exactly — patient, unhurried, always further. Out here there is nothing to reach and nothing to miss. That is the strange mercy of open water: it asks nothing of you but your attention, and returns the whole sky.

01 · SWELL

Old news, travelling.

The swell is a storm that ended a thousand miles ago and never got the message. It arrives under the hull as one long rolling breath — rise, and hold, and let go — the sea remembering weather you will never see.

02 · LIGHT

A thousand small dawns.

At golden hour the sun stops climbing and starts spilling. It gilds a wave-face, then lets it fall dark, then gilds the next — glitter scattered and re-scattered, a shimmering path laid down from the horizon to your feet.

03 · SILENCE

The loudest thing for miles.

No engine, no gulls. Only the slap and hush of water finding the boat and losing it again, over and over, unbothered. The loudest thing for miles is your own quiet attention, turned all the way up.

Somewhere ahead the light goes down and takes the day with it. The sea does not notice. Neither, out here, will you.
