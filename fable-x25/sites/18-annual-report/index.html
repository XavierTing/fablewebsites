01 / DELIVERY

Twelve months of supply, one unbroken line.

System delivery rose 3.9% to 412.0 billion litres. The hottest January on record pushed monthly demand to a peak of 39.6 billion litres — met in full, with treatment plants at 81% of rated capacity and no district placed on restriction.

383.2 BL OF 412.0 BL DELIVERED WAS METERED AND BILLED. THE REMAINDER IS NON-REVENUE WATER — LEAKAGE, FLUSHING AND FIREFIGHTING — TREATED IN CHAPTER 03.

02 / STORAGE

Twelve reservoirs, drawn to scale.

Combined storage closed the year at 859 gigalitres — 69.3% of capacity, equal to 2.1 years of demand. Eleven reservoirs finished inside their operating bands. Redcrest fell below its 45% floor in March and remains under a supplementary transfer order.

03 / CONSUMPTION

Where the water went.

Metered consumption grew 3.2% to 383.2 billion litres. Six of eight districts drew more than the year prior; the Ironquay industrial estate alone accounted for more than a third of the systemwide increase. Westfield and Greyfern fell on the strength of pressure management and 41,000 retrofitted meters.

Auditor's
Note

In our opinion, the volumetric and financial statements of Atlas Water Authority present fairly, in all material respects, the operations of the Authority for the year ended 30 June 2026, in accordance with the Public Utilities Reporting Standard (PURS-9).

We draw attention, without qualification, to Note 11: non-revenue water of 28.8 BL (7.0% of delivery) exceeds the Authority's licensed target of 6.5%. Management's remediation program is disclosed in Note 12 and is, in our assessment, adequately funded. Metered volumes were verified against telemetry on a sample of 1,900 connections with a maximum observed variance of 0.3%. Figures throughout this report cross-cast: delivery of 412.0 BL equals metered consumption of 383.2 BL plus non-revenue water of 28.8 BL.
