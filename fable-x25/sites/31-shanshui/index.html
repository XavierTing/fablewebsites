論 · On the tradition
山水

Not a picture of a place, but a place to be entered — mountains and water offered to the eye as a walk one takes with the whole body.

留白liú bái · leaving white

What is not painted carries as much as what is. Mist, river, and sky are most often bare silk — untouched paper the eye reads as light, as distance, as breath. The Song masters trusted emptiness the way a musician trusts a rest. Restraint is the luxury; the void is not absence but room.

手卷shǒu juàn · the handscroll

A handscroll is never seen all at once. It is opened an arm's length at a time — unrolled from the right, re-rolled on the left — so the landscape arrives as a journey rather than a single view. You do not stand before it. You travel through it, one reach of the river at a time.

臥遊wò yóu · wandering while lying down

When the painter Zong Bing grew too old to climb, he hung his mountains on the wall so he might roam them from his couch. To read shān shuǐ is to travel without moving — a thousand li of peaks and mist crossed in the time it takes the eye to drift from one ridge to the next.
