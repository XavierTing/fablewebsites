Type is infrastructure. It should carry a paragraph the way a bridge carries a river — invisibly, and without ever asking to be thanked.

A.Cold, on purpose

“Cold set” once meant type composed without molten metal — photographic, then digital. We took the name as a discipline: every curve is cut on a screen and proofed on a screen, at the sizes it will actually be read.

B.Variable by default

Each family ships as a single variable file with continuous axes — weight, width, optical size. One file, an infinite number of instances, tuned live in the tester below rather than sold to you as forty separate weights.

C.Drawn to be used

Real figures. Real small caps. Fractions, tabular numerals, five stylistic sets. We build the boring, load-bearing glyphs first — the ones a working designer reaches for at 2 a.m. on deadline.

Field Notes / Issue 14
The engineers who fell in love with a curve

For eleven years the studio measured latency in milliseconds and beauty in almost nothing at all. Then a single terminal comma — set too tight, then too loose — cost them a morning and taught them the whole trade. A letter, they decided, is a machine for holding a reader’s attention just long enough. Not longer. The width of a stroke is a decision about time. Every ascender is a small promise that the sentence will, eventually, end. They rebuilt the face from the comma outward, proofed it at every optical size between a caption and a billboard, and shipped it cold — no lead, no heat, no apology.
